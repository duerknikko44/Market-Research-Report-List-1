<p><p>The electrolytic capacitor (E-Cap) market is divided into two main types: solid type and non-solid type. Solid electrolytic capacitors use a conductive polymer as the electrolyte, offering low ESR (equivalent series resistance) and improved reliability, making them suitable for high-frequency applications. Non-solid electrolytic capacitors, typically featuring liquid electrolytes, are widely used in power supplies and audio applications due to their high capacitance values and cost-effectiveness. Both types serve various applications across consumer electronics, automotive, and industrial sectors.</p></p>
