<p><p>The nanocrystalline diamond market comprises two main types: HPHT (High Pressure, High Temperature) and CVD (Chemical Vapor Deposition). HPHT involves synthesizing diamonds by mimicking natural geological conditions, resulting in diamonds with unique properties. CVD, on the other hand, uses gaseous precursors to deposit diamond films on substrates at lower temperatures, allowing for precise control over diamond characteristics. Both methods cater to diverse applications, including cutting tools, electronics, and biomedical devices, driving growth in various industrial sectors.</p></p>
