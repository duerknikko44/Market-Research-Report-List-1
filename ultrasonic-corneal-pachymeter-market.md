<p><ul><li>Contact Ultrasonic Corneal Pachymeter</li><li>Non-Contact Ultrasonic Corneal Pachymeter</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Ultrasonic Corneal Pachymeter market is categorized into two primary types: Contact Ultrasonic Corneal Pachymeters and Non-Contact Ultrasonic Corneal Pachymeters. Contact Ultrasonic Pachymeters require direct contact with the cornea to measure its thickness, offering precise readings but potentially causing patient discomfort. In contrast, Non-Contact Ultrasonic Pachymeters use a probe that does not touch the eye, enhancing patient comfort and reducing the risk of infection, though they may be slightly less accurate. Both types are essential for diagnosing and managing various corneal conditions.</p></p>
