<p><p>Lithium extraction from salt lake brine involves several methods, each catering to different market needs. The precipitation method uses chemical reactions to separate lithium salts. Adsorption captures lithium ions using specific materials. Electrodialysis employs electric currents to drive ionic movement, separating lithium effectively. Membrane separation uses selective barriers to isolate lithium. The calcination method involves heating to remove impurities, while solvent extraction relies on organic solvents to selectively extract lithium. Each method offers unique advantages in efficiency and cost.</p></p>
